Hardware-Enforced Governance

128 rigid safety axioms organized into eight 16-bit registers. Because these axioms are mapped isomorphically to logic gates on the HUC fabric, governance is deterministic and cannot be bypassed, tampered with, or updated without physically destroying the chip.

The D7 Sovereign register ensures an absolute physical kill-switch and human-override authority is maintained at the Most Significant Bit level.

Challenges & Limitations

The transition to an AI-native paradigm is accompanied by significant engineering challenges.

01

Hardware Constraints

Continuous multimodal inference risks thermal throttling and battery degradation. Requires breakthroughs in low-bit quantization and analog computing.

Critical
02

Model Hallucination

A hallucination in an Agentic OS can delete databases or misconfigure security. Demands rigorous reinforcement learning and verifiable reward modeling.

Extreme
03

OS-Level Security

Indirect Prompt Injection via benign documents can hijack the agent's logic. Requires impenetrable isolation between reasoning and execution.

Extreme
